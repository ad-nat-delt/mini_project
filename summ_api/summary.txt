- Wildfires in Canada are causing air quality alerts across the U.S., affecting areas from Maine to Minnesota, with warnings to stay indoors due to unhealthy air quality levels.
- Peter DeCarlo, an associate professor at Johns Hopkins University, explains that dry conditions and weather systems are channeling smoke into the U.S., with particulate matter concentrations reaching unhealthy levels, impacting respiratory, cardiovascular, and neurological health.
- Vulnerable groups include children, the elderly, and those with pre-existing health conditions. The situation may improve with changing weather patterns, but with climate change, such widespread air quality issues are expected to become more frequent.
