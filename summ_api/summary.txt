- Wildfires in Canada are causing air quality alerts across the U.S., with smoke affecting areas from Maine to Maryland to Minnesota, leading to warnings to stay indoors.
- Peter DeCarlo of Johns Hopkins University explains that dry conditions and weather systems are channeling smoke into the U.S., with particulate matter levels reaching unhealthy concentrations over ten times the annual average.
- Vulnerable groups include children, the elderly, and those with pre-existing health conditions. The situation may improve with changing weather systems, but with climate change, such incidents are expected to become more frequent.
